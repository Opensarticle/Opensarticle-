The Vitran-Peeq Postulate

Published on January 21, 2026 | By Anshik Pathak

Lead Researcher

FocusTheoretical Physics
FrameworkUnified Field Mechanics
Abstract: This paper introduces a hierarchical framework proposing that the physical universe emerges from a primordial substrate termed the Vitran Field. We define Peeq-nodes as localized high-frequency excitations within this field. Shifting away from particle-centric models, this work derives macroscopic solidity through the principle of Kinetic Persistence.

1. The Vitran Field (V)

The Vitran-Peeq Postulate suggests that what we perceive as "empty space" is actually a continuous, physical substrate under tension. This Vitran Field (V) serves as the fundamental medium for all energy propagation.

2. Peeq-Nodes: Vibrational Origin of Mass

Matter is not a collection of solid balls, but localized "pinches" or excitations in the field. These are Peeq-nodes. The mass of a particle is simply the frequency of its oscillation within the substrate.

m = (h · f_p) / c²

3. Kinetic Persistence & Solidity

To explain the sensation of physical barriers, we introduce Kinetic Persistence. At extremely high frequencies, a field oscillation creates a structural integrity that manifests as "solidity" to macroscopic observers. This explains how hollow atomic structures exhibit high bulk moduli.

4. The Speed of Light Formula

We derive the speed of light as a result of the field's internal mechanical properties, linked by Anshik's Constant (Ap):

c = √(T / Ap)

Conclusion

By moving from "Materialism" to "Field Mechanics," the Vitran-Peeq Postulate offers a unified view of the cosmos, providing a new mathematical path to bridge the gap between quantum states and general relativity.
