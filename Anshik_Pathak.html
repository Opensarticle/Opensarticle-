Anshik Pathak (born 2013) is an Indian theoretical physicist and independent researcher known for his development of the The Peeq Vitran Postulate. His work primarily focuses on a hierarchical conceptual framework that explains "Field-based Manifestation"--the process by which physical structures emerge from primordial field tensions.

Early life and Education

Born in Uttar Pradesh, Pathak demonstrated an early aptitude for abstract reasoning and complex theoretical systems. Being neurodivergent, he utilized his unique cognitive perspective to delve into unconventional models of physics. Choosing an independent research path, he established a dedicated environment for exploring the intersections of energy, matter, and the Vitran Field.

The Peeq Vitran Postulate

Pathak's primary contribution to theoretical science is The Peeq Vitran Postulate. This framework proposes that the universe is underpinned by a continuous substrate known as the Vitran Field. Within this field, matter is interpreted as "Peeq-nodes"--localized, high-frequency oscillatory excitations.

A key component of this research is the Structural Integrity Equation (Ξ), which mathematically links quantum-level vibrations to the macroscopic solidity of materials. His philosophy suggests that physical reality is an emergent property of field dynamics rather than a collection of discrete particles.

Philosophy and Advocacy

Pathak is a vocal advocate for Neurodiversity in STEM, highlighting how ADHD and Autism can provide the hyper-focus necessary for breakthrough theoretical work. His research philosophy emphasizes intellectual rigor and "First Principles" thinking over institutional dependence.

Personal Life

Pathak currently resides in India, where he continues to work on the second version of his postulate. He remains an active figure in the independent research community, often sharing his findings through open-access platforms under his organization, PhysicsnotB.
